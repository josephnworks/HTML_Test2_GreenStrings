Ferdie Knight

Website: www.ferdieknight.com

Ferdie Knight is a gritty, magnetic rock musician hailing from the Mangopare, Selwyn, Blossomland. Known for his weather-beaten leather jackets, tangled black curls, and voice like gravel soaked in bourbon, Ferdie exploded onto the indie rock scene with his extended play Firm EP. Raised on a sheep farm and steeped in old vinyl: Zeppelin, Neil Young, and The Datsuns; he writes anthems that blend rural loneliness with raw, urban energy.

Ferdie's music is laced with haunting lyrics about fractured love, storms, and long drives through nowhere. His live shows are equal parts sermon and reckoning, often ending with him bleeding from his guitar fingers and the crowd chanting his name like a prayer. A cult figure in the Southern Hemisphere and a rising myth everywhere else, Ferdie Knight doesn't just play rock—he lives it, breathes it, and burns in it.
